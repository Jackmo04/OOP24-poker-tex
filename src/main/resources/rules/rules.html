How to Play

The Basics

A game of Poker is composed of a series of hands. Each hand progresses as follows:

Two cards are dealt face down to each player and a total of five community cards are dealt face up during the four phases of the game. (see Hand Phases)

Each player seeks the best poker combination obtainable from the seven cards at their disposal (the five community cards plus their two personal cards). (see Combinations)

During each phase the players are allowed to make bets which will be added to a pot. The player who has the best combination at the end of the hand, or is the only one who hasn't folded, wins the pot. (see Betting)

The game continues until a player runs out of chips, at which point they are eliminated from the game. The last player remaining wins the game.

Hand Phases

Before each hand begins, two roles are assigned to two players: the small bilnd and the big blind.
The big blind is always the player to the left of the small blind, and these roles rotate clockwise after each hand.
These players are required to make a bet before the hand begins, in particular:

  • The small blind must bet half of the minimum bet
  • The big blind must bet the minimum bet

After these mandatory bets are made, the hand begins and progresses through four phases:
  1. Pre-flop
  2. Flop
  3. Turn
  4. River

1. Pre-flop

Each player is dealt two cards face down and the first round of betting begins.
The player to the left of the big blind is the first to act.

2. Flop

Three community cards are dealt face up and the second round of betting begins.
The small blind is the first to act.

3. Turn

A fourth community card is dealt face up and the third round of betting begins.
The small blind is the first to act.

4. River

A fifth and final community card is dealt face up and the fourth round of betting begins.
The small blind is the first to act.

Betting

During each phase, players are allowed to make bets. The following actions are allowed:

  • Check: If no one has bet yet, the player can check and pass the turn to the next player.
  • Call: The player bets enough chips to match the current highest bet.
  • Raise: The player bets more chips than the current highest bet.
  • Fold: The player discards their cards and forfeits the hand.
  • All-In: The player bets ALL of their remaining chips.
For example, if the current bet is 1000 chips, a player can call by betting 1000 chips, raise by betting more than 1000 chips, or fold by discarding their cards.

Each betting round proceeds in clockwise order and ends when all players have either folded or called the highest bet, meaning that if a player raises the current bet, the other players must call the new bet to continue playing. If a player goes all-in (so they are left with no chips), they can still win the hand even if they can't match the highest bets made by other players.

Combinations

The goal of the game is to obtain the best combination of five cards from the seven cards at your disposal.
The possible combinations are, in descending order of strength:

  1. Royal Flush: A sequence (A, K, Q, J, 10) all of the same suit
  2. Straight Flush: Any five consecutive cards of the same suit
  3. Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank
  4. Full House: Three cards of the same rank and two cards of another rank
  5. Flush: Five cards of the same suit
  6. Straight: Five consecutive ranked cards of different suits
  7. Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank
  8. Two Pair: Two cards of the same rank and two cards of another rank
  9. Pair: Two cards of the same rank
  10. High Card: The highest ranked card of the seven cards

If two players have the same combination, the player with the highest card contributing to the combination wins. For example, if a player has a pair of Aces and another player has a pair of Kings, the player with the pair of Aces wins.

Example

The table

  • On the bottom you have buttons to perform actions, a display of the amount of chips you have left and your cards fot this hand.
  • On the left, top and right you can see the other players' chips and their cards (face down).
  • In the middle you can see the community cards and the pot.

Pre-flop

  • Two players are assigned the big blind and small blind roles, which are identified by the letters "SB" and "BB" to the right of their chips.
  • The other players' actions are displayed to the left of their chips.
  • When it's your turn to act, the action buttons on the bottom will activate.

Flop / Turn / River

  • Depending on the current phase, a certain number of the community cards will be turned face up.
  • Each betting round will begin starting from the small blind (SB) and proceed clockwise.

Raising

  • If you want to raise, you'll have to input the amount of chips you want to bet in the box on the bottom, press Enter and finally press the Raise button.

Showdown

  • After the River phase, the players who haven't folded will show their cards.
  • In this exampe, the player on the left won because they had a Flush, while the user only had a Pair
  • The chips in the pot are then given to the hand's winner
